Capture Data Without Capturing A Critter

OpenHouse Station

The OpenHouse Station is the first data collecting trap that isn't a trap. This device can collect multiple forms of data from what ever small mammal enters. The mammal is free to enter and leave the device when it likes.

$100

Compact Design

The OpenHouse Station was developed in mind for capturing data from small mammals, so the design had to reflect that. The device is roughly 10"x5"x4". While very small, the OpenHouse Station is very durable. The device comprises a small scale as well as a camera. The device is seperated into two internal compartments. The first houses the scale and camera, while the second houses the battery, wires, and the circuits.

Innovative Technology

Innovation drives science, so we have spent many years developing a state of the art device to help in these efforts. The floor of the internal chamber is an extremely accurate scale. Due to the small nature of these creatures, it is important to collect very accurate weight measurements to differentiate the minute differences between each mammal. The data collected by these devices can be sent directly to our cloud-based servers allowing people across the world to interact with the data.

Harmless to Animals

Animal safety was a priority in developing the OpenHouse Station. This device was created to collect data from these small mammals without capturing them. The device is made from a durable polycaprolactone polysester. This material is non-toxic and cleaner than commonly used plastics. The internal compartment contains a small amount of water and food to attract the animal. The device never traps the creature, allowing it to enter and leave when it pleases.
